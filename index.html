Project Overview

This project analyzes how product positioning within a retail store affects sales performance. Using a comprehensive dataset with variables like product placement (Endcap, Aisle, Front of Store), foot traffic, consumer demographics, and pricing, we built interactive Tableau dashboards to uncover actionable insights.

The analysis covers 3 product categories — Clothing, Electronics, and Food — across multiple store positions and promotion strategies.

Key Insights

👕
Clothing Leads Sales

Clothing has the highest average sales volume (1,830.1), outperforming Electronics (1,748.6) and Food (1,727.7) across all store positions.

🏪
Front of Store Wins

Products placed at the Front of Store consistently show higher sales volume (1,923.7 for Clothing), confirming that visibility drives purchases.

🚶
Foot Traffic Impact

Low foot traffic areas show high avg sales (1,900.5), suggesting targeted placement strategies can overcome traffic disadvantages.

🏷
Promotions Boost Sales

Products with promotions average ~1,779 in sales vs ~1,757 without, with a consistent uplift pattern across all categories.

💰
Price vs Competitor

Our prices (~27–29) are slightly higher than competitor prices (~25) across all categories, yet sales remain strong due to brand positioning.

👪
Demographics Balanced

Sales are evenly distributed across College Students (450K), Families (443K), Seniors (444K), and Young Adults (432K).
